User journey v2 — multimodal intake

Updated flow with context enrichment fork at step 3

1
Persona selection
Content Creator or Product Manager. Sets available agent crew and output artifacts.
2
Topic input
Free text. Broad or specific — the system handles both.
3
Context enrichment optional, multimodal
📄 Upload documents
PDFs, Word docs, images, slides. System extracts context and anchors research to your existing material.
🔗 Social handle
Instagram, LinkedIn, X. System reads existing content, detects voice and audience, identifies what's missing.
🌐 Website URL
Scrapes and analyzes your site. Research fills content gaps relative to what you've already published.
4
Clarification loop lightweight agent
Questions are now informed by uploaded context — if you gave a social handle, the system already knows your audience and skips asking. Fewer questions, smarter brief.
5
Source curation pause point
System surfaces vetted sources relevant to the topic + context. User selects. Uploaded docs are automatically included as additional sources.
6
Artifact selection
Pick what you want out. Only selected artifacts are generated — no waste, lower cost.
7
Agent crew runs LangGraph orchestration
Retrieval → Analysis → Synthesis → Report Builder. Multimodal where needed — image analysis, URL scraping, doc parsing all feed into the same pipeline.
8
Structured output delivered
Persona-specific artifacts with citations. Context-aware — output reflects your voice, your audience, your gaps.
Why multimodal intake changes everything
Most research tools give generic output. By understanding who you already are (your content, your voice, your gaps), this system produces research that's personalised to your specific situation — not just the topic.
